- read, fork, modify, and redistribute the source for **noncommercial** purposes

If your use is **commercial**, you need a separate written license from the
copyright holder before shipping. This page describes when that applies and
how to get one.

## What counts as commercial use

A use is **commercial** if any of the following are true:

1. You or your company derive (or expect to derive) revenue, fees, or
   in-kind compensation from a product, service, internal tool, or hosted
   offering that incorporates LLM Workbench (in source or compiled form),
   or any substantial part of it.
2. You bundle LLM Workbench (or anything derived from it) into a commercial
   product, SaaS, or paid distribution.
3. You operate LLM Workbench as part of revenue-generating internal
   operations of a for-profit entity, including paid R&D and consulting
   engagements.

Public-benefit uses listed in the PolyForm Noncommercial license
(charities, schools, public research, public health/safety, environmental
protection, government) are **not** commercial uses, regardless of funding.

## How to get a commercial license

- **Email**: open a GitHub Issue titled `commercial license inquiry` on
  this repository, or contact the copyright holder via the email address
  listed on their GitHub profile (https://github.com/roymcfarland).
- Include: company name, intended use case, expected scale (users, runs
  per month), and a primary technical contact.
- The default offer is a paid, perpetual, non-transferable license per
  product or per legal entity. Royalty-bearing terms are also available
  for higher-volume integrations.

Until a written commercial license is signed, **commercial use is not
permitted**, even if a fork is publicly available.

## Contributions and dual licensing

Contributors retain copyright in their contributions but, by submitting a
pull request, agree to the inbound license terms in
[`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md). Those terms grant the project
maintainer the right to **dual-license** the codebase (e.g. to ship the
same code under PolyForm Noncommercial publicly and under a paid
commercial license to specific customers). This means external
contributions cannot block the maintainer's ability to monetize the
project, and contributions remain attributable to their authors.
